Project Overview

In today's competitive telecom industry, customer retention is crucial. This project applies machine learning techniques to predict customer churn, enabling telecom companies to take proactive measures to retain valuable customers.

Using historical data on customer behavior, billing information, and service usage, our models can identify patterns that indicate a customer is likely to leave, allowing for targeted retention strategies.

Research Problem

Customer churn remains a major challenge for telecommunication companies, directly impacting revenue, market share, and long-term sustainability. Traditional methods often fail to capture the complex nature of customer behavior and are typically reactive.

This project addresses the question: "How can machine learning techniques be used to develop accurate, interpretable, and scalable models for predicting customer churn in the telecommunication industry?"

Project Objectives

The main objective is to develop robust predictive models that accurately identify customers at risk of churning.

  • Analyze customer behavioral, demographic, and service usage data
  • Compare multiple machine learning algorithms for churn prediction
  • Evaluate model performance using key classification metrics
  • Identify influential features contributing to customer churn
  • Support proactive decision-making within customer retention frameworks
